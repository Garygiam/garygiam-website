## Programme-Level Rules

### Narrative Inheritance Rule

Every page must inherit the same five-layer public narrative:

1. The Problem
2. The Category
3. The Operating System
4. The Proof
5. The Future

Not every page must express all five layers equally.

But every page must strengthen the same category understanding rather than creating a new one.

### Category Validation Rule

Every future section, component, proof asset, article, and CTA must pass this test:

`Does this strengthen the public understanding of GEH as an entrepreneurial operating system?`

If the answer is no, it must be:

- removed
- reframed
- deferred
- or kept off the site

### Non-Competition Rule

No page may present Gary primarily as:

- only a founder of multiple companies
- only a consultant
- only a personal brand
- only a portfolio owner

Those roles may appear, but they must remain subordinate to the category architecture:

Gary is the architect of `GEH`, and the ventures are expressions of the system.

### Frozen-Narrative Rule

PB13 may not:

- expand the acronym publicly
- invent a rival page-level message hierarchy
- soften the problem-first structure into a generic founder website
- replace `GEH` with a safer but weaker framing

## Programme Dependencies

### Core Dependency Chain

`Homepage` comes first because it establishes the public category frame.

All later pages depend on that frame being publicly legible.

### Page Dependency Logic

- `About` depends on Homepage category framing so Gary’s story reads as the origin of the operating system rather than a generic biography
- `Companies` depends on Homepage and About because the ventures must be read as expressions of `GEH`, not as a founder portfolio
- `Media / Authority` depends on the earlier narrative layers so public proof validates the operating system rather than detached founder credibility
- `Insights` depends on the prior layers because publishing should compound the category rather than introduce parallel thought leadership
- `Contact` depends on all previous pages because conversion must feel like entry into the system, not a generic founder inbox

PB13 should pause at these major checkpoints:

- after the PB13 programme design

### Knowledge Harvest Checkpoints

Each tranche must capture:

Each tranche should include a promotion review for narrative assets and patterns.

The goal is to classify whether an implementation discovery is:

- page-local only
- reusable across GaryGiam.com
- a future GEH pattern candidate

Promotion review in PB13 does not change `GEH Narrative Architecture v1`.

It only evaluates what implementation evidence suggests may deserve reuse or future validation.

## PB13 Programme Overview

## Tranche 1 — Homepage

### Mission

Make the homepage the pilot public implementation of `GEH` as a category.

It should be the first page that clearly leaves visitors with the impression that Gary Giam is building an entrepreneurial operating system that continuously creates stronger organizations.

### Objectives

- convert the homepage from ecosystem-led founder authority into category-led headquarters positioning
- make the problem legible before the category definition appears
- define `GEH` publicly without relying on acronym expansion
- explain Gary as the architect of the operating system
- frame the ventures as expressions of the system
- make proof support the category
- make future participation feel like engagement with `GEH`, not generic founder contact

### Narrative Role

The homepage answers:

- what problem exists
- why `GEH` must exist
- why Gary is building it
- why the ventures matter as proof
- why the visitor should care now

### Detailed Homepage Design

#### Narrative Sequence

The homepage should implement the frozen architecture in this order:

1. Problem
2. Category
3. Operating System
4. Proof
5. Future / Participation

This should not be presented as five mechanical labels.

It should feel like one coherent progression.

#### Homepage Section Roles

##### Section 1 — Hero / Problem Entry

Purpose:

- establish visible founder presence
- introduce the recognizable organizational pain
- signal that the site is about a bigger system, not merely a founder profile

Required narrative outcome:

- visitors should feel the site is addressing a real recurring problem, not just presenting achievements

##### Section 2 — Category Introduction

Purpose:

- define `GEH` as the new category
- create the mental shift from “multiple ventures” to “entrepreneurial operating system”

Required narrative outcome:

- visitors should leave this section understanding that `GEH` is the frame that explains the rest of the site

##### Section 3 — Operating-System Logic

Purpose:

- explain how `GEH` works differently
- connect Gary to the system as architect rather than as a founder with many titles

Required narrative outcome:

- the operating principles of `GEH` should become legible without turning into manifesto copy

##### Section 4 — Proof Through Venture Expression

Purpose:

- show that the ventures are expressions of `GEH`
- use proof to validate the system rather than merely listing entities

Required narrative outcome:

- visitors should see organizational variety as evidence of system logic, not narrative drift

##### Section 5 — Authority / Proof Layer

Purpose:

- use recognition, media, and founder proof to support `GEH` credibility

Required narrative outcome:

- proof should answer “why believe this system works?”

##### Section 6 — Future / Engagement

Purpose:

- explain where the category is going
- make engagement feel like entry into the system

Required narrative outcome:

- visitors should know why `GEH` matters beyond the present site and how to respond to it

### Homepage Dependencies

- frozen GEH Narrative Architecture v1
- current authority vault
- current ecosystem content model
- existing recognition and media assets

### Homepage Expected Outcomes

- homepage becomes the public headquarters of the category
- founder authority becomes subordinate to category architecture rather than competing with it
- ecosystem section becomes system proof rather than venture listing
- authority section becomes system validation rather than standalone recognition
- contact intent becomes category participation rather than generic outreach

### Homepage Success Criteria

- a first-time visitor understands that `GEH` is the central public category
- a first-time visitor understands that Gary is building an entrepreneurial operating system
- the ventures read as outputs or expressions of the system
- trust and proof reinforce the category rather than distract from it
- no major homepage section introduces a parallel founder-only or portfolio-only narrative
