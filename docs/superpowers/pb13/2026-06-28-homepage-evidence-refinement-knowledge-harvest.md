## What Held

- Problem recognition before category naming: the homepage became stronger once the opening moved from abstract explanation to a recognizable organizational reality of momentum without strength.
- Inevitability over persuasion: adding the failure-of-current-approaches bridge made `GEH` feel more like the logical conclusion to a known problem than a founder-defined concept.
- System-led proof clarity: the ventures became more useful when each one explicitly stated what organizational strengthening it demonstrated.
- Organizational-strengthening evidence: verified public transformation signals improved credibility more effectively than adding more generic recognition or association proof.

## What Strengthened

- The bridge from observable reality to category necessity: the hero now names fragility and failure conditions before introducing `GEH`, which improves category inevitability.
- The understanding of Gary as architect: the homepage now keeps Gary positioned as the builder of the operating system rather than drifting back toward a multi-company founder reading.
- The understanding of ventures as proof: venture cards now explain what stronger capability, structure, or outcomes each expression validates.

- Narrative patterns reusable across GaryGiam.com: lead with recognizable organizational reality, then show why current approaches fail before naming the category.
- Proof patterns reusable across GaryGiam.com: make every proof element answer both what it proves about `GEH` and what organizational strengthening it proves.
